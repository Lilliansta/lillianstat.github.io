A Taste of Everything


Malaysia holds a wide range of diverse cultures with many residents understanding and speaking multiple languages at a time.


Apart from the scourching heat, I had a great time trying new foods. The combination of vibrant city life and beautiful nature sceneries makes Malaysia even more captivating to visit.

Batu Caves


Located in Selangor, Batu Caves is famous for its 272 colourful stairs and giant golden statue of the god of Hindu, known as Lord Murugan meaning proud and tall at the foot of the stairs.


The temple that sits inside has been there since 1920, allowing Hindus from all over to come celebrate festivals and pray as a community.


Watch out for the cheeky monkeys that live around the caves, many like to steal the loose items you own.


Chinatown Kuala Lumper


Located on Petaling Street, Chinatown is a large market with many fashion, accessories and food stalls. Where You can experience authentic Chinese-Malaysian foods such as:

  • Hokkien Mee(Thick yellow noodles)
  • Hainanese Chicken Rice
  • Buk Kut Teh(A soup with herbs, spices and pork ribs)
  • Char Kway Teow(Fried noodle dish)
